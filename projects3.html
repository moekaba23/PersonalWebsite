URBAN HEALTH PLAN

Case Study

Urban Health Plan is a system of community health centers in New York City. As a patient myself that uses the health centers, I find their web portal for patients to be very outdated and difficult to use. Hence came the idea to redesign it.

The Problem

  • Outdated and clunky interface.
  • Inconsistent design choices.
  • Bad information architecture and hierarchy.

The Solution

I embarked on a full redesign of Urban Health Plan's patient web portal where I improved the user experience by simplifying the interface and the information architecture.

Current Web Portal

Research Process

User Interviews

I conducted in person interviews with 6 patients at two different clinic branches. The user interviews helped us to get an idea of what our users were looking for and to gather as much information from them as possible before moving forward.

One complaint that came up consistently during the interviews was the inability to schedule doctor’s appointments through the web portal. Patients can only schedule through phone call or in person walking. Therefore implementing a new feature that allows patients to schedule their doctor’s appointments through the web portal was crucial.
